Unused Elements

Page 2 of our Unused Elements page, detailing even more information on early revisions of the game. Click here to return to Page 1.

Wrecking Kids

According to the original site for Furballs -

Furball Village contains a house for each of the Furballs, to which their children will return - and wreak havoc! - as the player rescues them.

Of course, in the final game, only a few babies appear, and they certainly don't wreak havoc. In fact, they don't even return to their homes, save for the ones who offer you a game to play. It's possible this was just misleading info, but hey - It was mentioned early enough in production for it to have been planned.

Mutated Winnie



Winnifred goes through the portal to The Bad Place already mutated. In the final game, she goes through as her normal, unmutated self, grasping Frederick in her paws.

Baby Firefox Plushie



In an earlier release of the game, the baby firefox plush in the Nursery of Dinos Upstairs was not holding the Exit sign. This was likely changed to make sure people knew they were heading out of the level as opposed to another room.

Boss Attack Names



On the left, boss attacks were going to have names for each of their attacks. For example, Gwynth's fire attack was given the name 'Roof Breath'. There was also a health bar for you to see how well you were doing. This was likely removed for consistency, since regular enemies have never been seen with a health bar. On the right, Tweek's Telepoint is on the field as opposed to in the helicopter.

The Undermill Beaver



Beaver placeholder in the Undermill. Not much else to say here.

Furball Village



Early signposts, still using the Furballs name. The name Furball Village is also present in the game demos, despite being officially regarded as Fur Fighters by this point.

Early Crocodile



See more images of this early version of the game at the bottom of this page. Here, however, the Crocodile has a noticeable frown.

Early Bungalow Texture, Anatat Foliage



An early texture for Bungalow, which resembles his concept art. Also, second image shows Anatat Tatanatat's foliage in Fur Fighter Village. It also looks as though the lake is filled in. On the far right, a very early version of Rico's home which appears to be... Empty.

Early Juliette Texture



Juliette's eyes were originally a rather beautiful sapphire blue. It's a shame they changed it, really. Also, second image shows the open cave seen in the demo.

Early Chang Texture



Chang's eyes were also a bit different. The pupil looked much smaller. Also, note the early areas here.

Happy Fur Fighters



As shown in an early video (seen below), an easy way to see how far along the game is can be seen by the health monitor - The early version features the cast with extremely happy faces, normally seen only when a can of Pet Yums is obtained. Or, as is the case in some of these images, the lack of babies. Also, note that Bungalow, Chang & Juliette all have their early textures here.
